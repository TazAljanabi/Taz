The Roots

Drawing by D.Kula

During my life, I have met so some people I call The Roots. The Roots are the people who supported me, provided me with everything I need to grow and be greater, the people who helped me stay put in the face of storms, unlike the branches that can break, unlike the leaves, which change with the weather and fall. The Roots stay put. The Roots remain. Some of my Roots were high school friends, some were high school teachers, and I clearly can justify the positive impact they had on me. One of my strongest Roots was my English language teacher, the great Mr. Wael Al Khamees. He helped in the creation of my strong English foundation compared to my peers, he nurtured my core with the love of English and made it fun to learn, he had a unique way of teaching that made his students wait impatiently for his class. He is my favorite teacher and without him, I would not have had the opportunity to be any good at English,. Meeting him was an opportunity I am very thankful for.

Just like the tides of a rigid ocean my high school

three years have passed as I dropped the sails pitch heading toward my destination three years of explorations in an adventurous journey that shaped and sculpted the person I am today.

During my journey, I was always optimistic and I always believed that I will see the sunlight up in the sky again, I was running from pain when a day had come when I realized that no one could outrun pain. I believe that life can be a tragedy, but it is also precious, sweet, and extraordinary. I knew that under all the pressure and weight I had gone through, from losing the love of my life and suffering from depression to overcoming it, I was being created to be the luminous star that one day will light the way of everyone in need, and the only way I know to honor my Roots is to keep on growing.
